The Stablecoin Advantage

The Stablecoin Advantage

Stablecoins like BOB offer a solution to the volatility that is often associated with cryptocurrencies.

BOB is intended for everyday use when stability is important, such as sending and receiving a salary, transferring money abroad, fundraising, or purchasing goods and services.

With BOB, affordable and predictable transactions are processed world-wide within minutes.

What sets BOB apart?

Advanced Stability
  • Concentrated liquidity provisions within a tight range create a stable foundation.
  • Base stability is inherited from USD-pegged stablecoins while parameter settings provide adjustability when needed.
  • The inventory distribution model means all BOB in circulation is 100% backed by on-chain assets.
Optional Privacy
  • The zkBob application removes barriers for a seamless user experience.
  • Single-token payments with BOB (no native tokens needed) streamline deposits, transfers and withdrawals.
  • Compliance features such as limits and AML measures prevent illicit funds from entering the protocol.
Connected Ecosystem
  • Multichain, multifaceted DeFi integrations provide broad and varied options for users.
  • Invoicing integrations for employee salaries make BOB payroll setup simple for companies and DAOs.
Easy Accessibility
  • BOB is readily available on common DeFi platforms and easily bridged between multiple chains.
  • The BOB CDP application lets users mint BOB using various on-chain assets.
Audited Security
  • A security audit by industry leader ChainSecurity provides peace of mind when interacting with BOB.
  • Increased security and safety for private transactions with AML compliance integrations.
Layered Governance
  • Slow and fast-track processes mitigate risks while supporting nimble development and feature advancement.
  • BOB DAO formation is planned to decentralize BOB management, ensuring the protocol is protected by and for the stablecoin community.

Self-Sustaining Protocol

Mechanisms such as idle capital compounding, fee surplus collection, and lost token retrieval allow the BOB protocol to support operations while optimizing costs and features for users. 100% on-chain collateral and a transparent process for maintaining stability make BOB the reliable choice for anyone looking to securely transfer value on the blockchain.

FAQ

How can I benefit from holding BOB?
Beyond the normal benefits of holding stablecoins, BOB users and holders will share in future revenue generated by the protocol. This will come in the form of earned interest from idle capital and lost token recovery fees. While most stable protocols keep these fees for themselves, with BOB these fees will be distributed back to the community. The method and timing of these distributions is still in development, and a governance process will ultimately decide the specifics of this program. Stay tuned for more info as the BOB ecosystem matures.
How are BOB and zkBob related?
BOB is a stablecoin built for everyday transactions. It can be used similarly to USDC, Tether, DAI and other stables to preserve value and avoid volatility.‍zkBob is a standalone application for private transactions. It only accepts BOB, which gives BOB an optional privacy advantage for use cases like private salary, anonymous donations, DAO payments and more. When used with zkBob, BOB transfers are completely private. See zkbob.com for more information
How many blockchains is BOB on?
BOB is currently available on Polygon, Optimism, Arbitrum, Ethereum, and Binance Smart Chain. Future governance decisions will determine if BOB is added to additional chains. The contract address for BOB is the same on every chain where it is deployed: 0xB0B195aEFA3650A6908f15CdaC7D92F8a5791B0B‍zkBob is currently only available on Polygon. If you wish to use zkBob, you can bridge BOB from other chains to Polygon using Li.Fi.
Is BOB code audited?
Yes! ChainSecurity delivered their security audit of BOB and zkBob contracts on January 5, 2023. It is available here -> https://chainsecurity.com/security-audit/zkbob-smart-contracts/
How is BOB inventory managed?
BOB inventory levels are managed via a streamlined governance and multisig approval process. A governance proposal to adjust inventory is discussed and approved/denied by the acting governance board. Note that BOB is still in beta and a wider and more decentralized governance process is planned for the future.‍ New BOB is added or removed from inventory based on current supply and anticipated demand. BOB stored in inventory is not in active circulation, only BOB which has been purchased from the inventory is in active circulation. All BOB in active circulation is backed 1x1 by stable assets used to purchase it from inventory.
How does BOB remain stable?
BOB maintains its stable value thanks to something called “concentrated liquidity.” Introduced in Uniswap v3, this concept allows liquidity to be provided in very narrow ranges. In the case of BOB, BOB inventory liquidity is paired with other well-known stable tokens (predominantly USDC) and provided to Uniswap v3.‍ Since USDC/BOB liquidity is only provided within an extremely tight range, BOB remains stable relative to USDC within that small window. The primary risk to stabilization is related to paired stablecoins, for example if USDC were to lose its peg BOB stability could also be impacted. Parameter tuning and dynamic pricing algorithms are in development to further protect BOB stability.
